Team Assistant

Our client is looking for an energetic team player to join their very prestigious yachting house based in Fort Lauderdale.  
The position is Team Assistant - this means you work with the entire team. Yacht Management, Yacht Brokerage, Charter Brokerage - that's right, you will be exposed to all of the aspects of a properly run yachting house.
Your daily duties are not glamorous (at least in the beginning), you will answer phones, make appointments (no cold calling), send and receive mail (for the office team and for the yachts under management), order pens and paper, keep the coffee machine going, keep the champagne cooler filled, make plane and hotel reservations, keep a smile on your face and be a genuinely happy person getting a start in the land based part of yachting.
The skill set required is a fairly short list - but it is non-negotiable:
You are well organized - keep lists, check off items as done, add items as they come in.
You are genuinely cheerful - the days can get hectic, you will keep a smile on your face, even when you want to kick a puppy (ok, hopefully it is never quite that hectic).
You are pro-active (did the wine chiller get a little empty after the guest party last night - you get right on ordering replacements, are the paper towels running low - hello, Amazon - we need a delivery).
You follow directions, as the assistant you will be hit with a number of "out of the blue" requests - the owner of that new 100m yacht is coming to town for a quick visit - get him a reservation at that new hotel on the beach!
You show up ready to work a bit early (a little bit - think 10 minutes before the office opens, you are there). Late does not happen so much, but occasionally.
You have an eye for detail and hospitality - this is yachting, your presentation is upscale and always happy to meet / speak with owners, guests, mechanics, a/c repair teams, the janitor, etc.
Your English is fantastic - properly spoken, well written.
Other languages (especially French, Italian, Spanish) are a big plus, but not required.
The list of not-requirements is also short - but non-negotiable.
You do not spend your entire day on Instagram (Facebook, Twitter, etc.)
You do not look at your cell phone all day long.
You are never annoyed to meet new people.

Some of the other bits.  
You can be brand new to the working world, but you will have to have some kind of references (we check them) other than your family who can tell us about your work ethic and your disposition.
You have to be at least 21 - no joking, part of your job is keeping the liquor cabinet stocked and you will be re-filling glasses at some of the parties.
You don't need a college education, but your ability to speak, write, a bit of spreadsheet work, be train-able in CRM databases, all matters.
You are a natty dresser (look it up if you don't know what it means) - this is yachting, we are not wearing tuxedos and formal gowns (too often) but a real interest in presentation and fashion matters.
You have reliable transportation to downtown Fort Lauderdale / 17th Street / close yacht yards.
The wages and benefits are very good, the work environment is very nice and the stress level is truly low. 
If you think that Brokerage / Sales / Management / Support in the yachting world might be the right career for you - you must apply for this - it is the perfect jumping off point!
You must be US Legal (Passport or Green Card) to be considered
