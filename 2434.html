Chef - 46m


Head Chef - how do you feel about being part of a very tight onboard family? If you are professional about your position and a cheerful team player - we have a fantastic opportunity for you.

This team, described as being “yacht-proud” where they understand that the only job that is done is one that is done right, travels to a good mix of locations, both yachting hot-spots and places way off the beaten path. And they are looking for a great Head Chef to join their very tight team.

This completely private 45m motor yacht was launched last year and is owned by a family that not only says “please” and “thank you,” but that is described as “simply wonderful, down to earth owners.”

As with most great programs, the Head Chef is squarely in the center of the owner’s enjoyment of the yacht, and is just as critical to the crew’s welfare and joy.

The owner’s are very simple with their food needs. The team aboard has a year’s worth of food details / preferences from their use. You will be starting with a great step-up into the owner’s tastes! The family uses the yacht about 1 week a month (give or take), and when aboard their enjoyment is the number one priority (of course).

Crew food is just as important! Variety, attention to detail, and quality counts just as much for the crew as the owners. You will baking fresh daily, meals will go from high quality steak one day to fish tacos the next. There is no “per-head/per-day” food budget. But they are not wasteful - smart planning, high quality, variety and attention to needs is the basis for a great menu for them.

The galley has great windows - that’s right, you are not in a tank below the water-line, you actually have a view (we found this so uncommon that we asked the Captain for photos to prove it)!

No galley is perfect, this one has limited freezer space (again, planning is important) and the galley is designed as part of the crew mess. This does mean people in your space - if you require isolation in the galley, this will not fit you. Everybody is very busy during guest use, so generally not an issue with guests aboard, but without guests, you can expect people to be around.

The chef shares a cabin with a stew.

The entire crew is upbeat, happy, healthy professionals. They do not tolerate heavy drinkers or any drugs.  

Wages are good (we do not share in either direction), solid benefit package including insurance, 38 days vacation the first year (increases after the first year), two round-trip holiday flights, training budget for crew with tenure. Oh, and the crew do things together! Diving, watersports, tendering, hiking, exploring - all good if you enjoy it!
