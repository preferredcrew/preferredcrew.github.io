Third Stew - 38m


Our client has an opportunity for a newer (not green) Third Stew to join their 42m Charter yacht.
The program is dual season – Mediterranean / Caribbean and runs for the enjoyment of the owners and does a bit more than average chartering. The owners are lovely people. They engage with the crew (so you must be comfortable speaking with people), they are happy folks who enjoy a good laugh and treat the crew with respect.
The Chief Stew is a great communicator. She outlines the work, the goals, and the challenges on a daily and weekly basis. When the work is done (properly) it is done. She does not look for busy work – but do know, as a full on working yacht, there is always a good list of appropriate to-dos.
As a Third, your responsibilities are 80% Housekeeping and Laundry. You will get a little service, and a bit more as you progress with the program.
They want to see somebody who has already has some yachting experience for two reasons – the main is that they want to know you are already tuned in to the daily work level it takes to work on a yacht (no Instagram/FB while working), and they want to be able to check your skills with your previous program (so if you left your only boat in a huff – this will likely not be the next move for you).
What do they offer – a professional program where you will build your skills. An opportunity to grow your career. Travel, as a two-season yacht you will be all over the Mediterranean and all over the Caribbean. When they charter, tips are split equally! Vacation is 42 days a year. Also, their program is very planned out. They know well in advance where they will be and what they will be doing – this allows the crew to actually make some plans for a life.
The requirements for consideration: STCW Basic and Medical fit for duty. One season of yachting experience aboard a yacht (2-3 days of daywork on a number of yachts will not work for them). Any nationality is fine, but English must be spoken at “Fluent” or better. Female only as the cabin is shared – another thing your references will be asked is how clean you kept your cabin. Tattoos (non-offensive and small) are not a problem, but absolutely no smoking or vaping is permitted. A pre-employment drug test and random drug tests are expected.
The position will not open until August – so if you are on a summer gig, or need to give appropriate notice this is not a problem.
